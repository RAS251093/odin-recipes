Scones

Description

These scones are the perfect treat for a summertime afternoon tea! Eat them plain, with butter, or topped with jam and clotted cream in whichever order you prefer. Recipe makes 7 - 8.

Equipment Required

  • Baking tray, lined with baking paper
  • 6 cm round cutter
  • Large mixing bowl
  • Measuring jug
  • Weighing scales
  • Oven

Ingredients

  • Self raising flour (250 g)
  • Caster sugar (50 g)
  • Unsalted butter, chilled, cubed (100 g)
  • Medium egg (1)
  • Milk (100 mL)
  • Salt (a pinch)
  • Handful of sultanas (optional)

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 220 °C/200 °C fan/Gas 7.
  2. Mix together the self-raising flour and a pinch of salt to the mixing bowl.
  3. Add the cubed butter and rub in with the tips of your fingers until the mixture resembles fine crumbs.
  4. Stir in the caster sugar.
  5. Measure out the milk into the measuring jug.
  6. Crack the egg into the jug, and beat until combined.
  7. Stir the egg and milk mixture into the crumbs until a soft, but not wet dough forms (you may not need all the liquid).
  8. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface.
  9. Knead lightly and work in the sultanas if using.
  10. Roll out into a rectangle, about 2 cm thick.
  11. Using the round cutter, cut into as many scones as possible, placing them onto the lined baking tray.
  12. Brush the scones with a little extra milk, or any leftover egg/milk mixture.
  13. Bake in the oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the scones are well risen and a pale, golden-brown colour.
  14. Once removed from the oven, lift onto a wire rack and allow to cool.
  15. Enjoy warm or cold, served with strawberry jam and clotted cream, or just plain!
